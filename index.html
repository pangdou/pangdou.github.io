Kaiming He 何恺明

  Research Scientist

  Facebook AI Research (FAIR), Menlo Park, CA

 

I joined Facebook AI Research (FAIR) in 2016 as a Research Scientist. Formerly I was with Microsoft Research Asia (MSRA), which I joined in 2011 after receiving my PhD. My research interests are in computer vision and deep learning.

I am a recipient of the Best Paper Award in CVPR 2009, CVPR 2016, and ICCV 2017 (Marr Prize), and the Best Student Paper Award in ICCV 2017. I and colleagues developed Deep Residual Networks (ResNets), currently the prevalent architecture in computer vision. Applications of ResNets also include machine translation, speech synthesis, speech recognition, and AlphaGo.


According to Google Scholar Metrics, as of June 2017:

  • "Deep Residual Learning for Image Recognition" is the most cited paper published in CVPR 2016.

  • "Faster R-CNN: Towards Real-Time Object Detection with Region Proposal Networks" is the most cited paper published in NIPS 2015.

  • "Delving Deep into Rectifiers: Surpassing Human-Level Performance on ImageNet Classification" is the most cited paper published in ICCV 2015.
